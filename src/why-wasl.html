Why WASL

Consolidate your stack. Run at the edge.

Platform teams are being asked to govern APIs, AI applications, event streams, integrations, partners, and agents at the same time. WASL brings those surfaces into one gateway platform so governance scales with the business instead of multiplying tools.

The Challenge

The Cost of Tool Sprawl

As organizations adopt APIs, event-driven architectures, and AI, the traffic perimeter fractures. Separate tools create operational drag and inconsistent governance:

01

Observability Gaps

Scattered logs, metrics, and traces across independent API management, AI proxies, and event streams make distributed debugging nearly impossible.

02

Policy Duplication

Rewriting authentication, rate-limiting, and validation rules in four separate systems introduces security risks and policy drift.

03

High Cloud Overhead

Running separate products for API, AI, event, MCP, and integration traffic increases cost, staffing needs, and operational complexity.

WASL difference

Designed for the next generation of digital traffic.

WASL is not a proxy with a few add-ons. It is a platform layer for governance, integration, security, developer experience, and AI-era traffic control.

One traffic layer

Bring API products, AI calls, event streams, agent tools, and backend integrations under a single governance model instead of operating disconnected control points.

Consistent control: Apply identity, quotas, validation, and auditability everywhere Shared visibility: Understand traffic behavior across APIs, AI, events, and integrations Lower tool sprawl: Reduce overlapping gateways, portals, policy engines, and proxies

Integration built in

WASL connects traffic governance with backend orchestration. Teams can transform, enrich, and route requests without pushing every integration into a separate middleware layer.

Composable services: Combine APIs, events, tools, and backend calls safely Governed connectors: Access data systems, queues, search, storage, and SaaS services Traceable automation: Keep integration behavior visible to platform teams

Resilient enterprise operations

WASL separates management workflows from live traffic serving so platform teams can manage releases, policy, and observability without putting business traffic at risk.

Traffic continuity: Keep services running while teams manage and review changes Versioned releases: Promote and roll back governed changes with confidence Hybrid placement: Run close to cloud, private, partner, or edge workloads

Extensible connector model

WASL is built to grow with enterprise connectivity needs. Add new systems and governed capabilities without turning the core gateway into a one-off custom project.

Connector marketplace: Standardize access to core systems and services Custom extensions: Add proprietary systems without breaking platform governance Trust controls: Govern connector rollout, compatibility, telemetry, and ownership
