FAQ

Frequently Asked Questions

Have questions about licensing, deployment models, or support tiers? Find answers below.

What is WASL's licensing model?

WASL operates on an open-core model. The core data-plane runtimes and the Plugin SDK are open-source and free to run. The advanced control-plane components, fleet synchronization engine, and specific AI governance policies are licensed under commercial terms.

Can I deploy WASL in our own private cloud?

Yes! All WASL runtimes are stateless containers. With our Enterprise plan, you can orchestrate hybrid fleets where runtimes register from within your private VPC or on-prem environments to our centralized control plane.

What is the difference between a direct proxy and a flow?

Direct Proxy mode forwards request streams directly to backend upstreams with minimal policy overhead (such as simple API keys). Flow mode compiles custom JavaScript logic (FlowJS) to choreograph multiple backends, translate headers, and invoke connector plugins.

How does the AI Gateway semantic cache work?

Available in our Enterprise tier, the semantic cache uses embedding models to parse incoming natural language prompts. If a semantically similar prompt was recently evaluated, the gateway serves the cached result directly, saving provider costs and reducing latency.
